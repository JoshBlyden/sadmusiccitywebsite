Today's Forcast: Some limelight for the lyricists! A look at the Grammy's brand new Song Writer of the Year award. The Grammy Awards have long been known for celebrating excellence in the field of music recording. However, a new award is being introduced this year that will celebrate excellence in songwriting - the Grammy Award for Songwriter of the Year, Non-Classical. The list of nominees for this award is impressive, and includes such major names as Desmond Child, James Fauntleroy, Natalie Hemby, Hillary Lindsey, Shane McAnally, Julia Michaels and Justin Tranter. So how did they make the list? In order to be eligible for the Songwriter of the Year award, a songwriter must have written or co-written at least three different songs that were released during the eligibility period. These songs must also have been commercially released as a single or as part of an album or EP, and they must have charted on one of the many Billboard charts, including the Hot 100, Billboard 200, and various genre-specific charts.
